Introduction

Pollution is the introduction of pollutants into the environment. These pollutants are called pollutants. Pollutants such as volcanic ash can be natural. They can also be produced by human activities such as factory-generated garbage and spills. Pollutants impair the quality of air, water and land.

Many of the things that are useful to humans cause environmental pollution. The car spits out contaminants from the exhaust pipe. Burning coal to generate electricity pollutes the air. Industries and households produce garbage and sewage that can pollute land and water. Insecticides (chemical toxins that kill weeds and insects) invade water bodies and damage wildlife.

All organisms, from single-cell microbes to blue whales, depend on the Earth's supply of air and water. Contamination of these resources threatens all life forms. Pollution is a global problem. Urban areas are usually more polluted than rural areas, but pollution can spread to remote, uninhabited areas. For example, pesticides and other chemicals have been found on the Antarctic ice sheet. In the middle of the North Pacific, a huge collection of fine plastic particles forms the so-called Great Pacific Garbage Patch.
